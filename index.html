Plants in the pacific ocean

  • Mushroom Coral

    Kingdom: Animalia
    Phylum: Cnidaria
    Class: Anthozoa
    Order: Scleractinia

    Various Mushroom Corals are known to live for more than 10 years. Mushroom Coral comes in different colors such as yellow, beige, white, purple, tan, and brown. Mushroom Corals prey on brine shrimp and small fish. Mushroom Coral mostly grows on the rocky reefs of the Caribbean and the Indo-Pacific Oceans.

  • Sargassum

    Kingdom: Chromista
    Phylum: Ochrophyta
    Class: Phaeophyceae
    Order: Fucales

    Sargassum floats in island-like masses that don’t attach to the seafloor. Once it washes ashore, it’s referred to as beach wrack. Floating mats of sargassum can stretch for miles across the ocean. Sargassum provides food and breeding grounds for birds, crabs and fish.Sargassum sinks to the ocean floor when it loses its buoyancy

  • Red Algae

    Kingdom: Plantae
    Phylum: Rhodophyta
    Class: florideophyceae
    Order: garcilariales

    If you swallow a drop of ocean water you’ll swallow thousands of microscopic algae, algae is the base of the marine food chain all plants have evolved from. Red Algae is a rich source of fiber, minerals, carbohydrates, protein, and enzymes. Red Algae forms an important part of the ecosystem. Later on Red Algae is consumed by many living organisms such as fish, worm, crustaceans, and even humans. Until now more than 6,000 species of Red Algae have been found.












Animals in the pacific ocean

  • Pacific sleeper shark

    Kingdom: Animal
    Phylum: Chordata
    Class: Chondrichthyes
    Order: Squaliformes

    Pacific sleeper sharks can grow up to 12.1 to 14.1 feet the longest sleeper shark 14 feet. They are also not known to attack humans they can live up to 40 years! They eat fishes, octopuses, squids, crabs, and tritons and ocasinaly sea lions and carrion.

  • Sea lion

    Kingdom: Animals
    Phylum: Chordata
    Class: Mammalia
    Order: Carnivora

    They live on average 20 years but some live for 30 they consume 5-8% of their body weight. There whiskers have a navigation system without them they would get lost. Sea Lions can not breath underwater but can hold their breath for 8-12 minutes. Sea lion are strong swimmers and can swim for nearly 20 miles in water. Sea Lions swim using their long fore flippers to produce power. Sea Lions hunt underwater and can dive depths up to 300 meters.

  • Sea otter

    Kingdom: Animal
    Phylum: Chordata
    Class: Mammalia
    Order: Carnivora

    They live up to 15-20 years there fur is the densest of any mammal they eat 25% of their body weight in food A sea otter’s tool if choice is typically a rock that can be used as a hammer or anvil to eat hard shelled prey












World dumping in the pacific ocean

  • World dumping

    An estimated 3.5 million tons of trash in this landfill that is resulting in whirling currents in the Pacific Ocean that pull trash and pollution into the ocean.By this the fish are also being affected it is estimated that fish are consuming 12,000 to 24,000 tons of plastic per year researchers collected that 141 fish of 27 different species and 9.2 present of them had plastic debris in their stomachs.
